Editors' Letter

This was hard. We won’t lie. Being the only longform vertical of Annenberg Media has been a process and a half. Capsule was handed over to us by Amina Niasse and Eddie Sun, the previous editors of the magazine. Questions like “Where do we start? What do we do? How do we begin?” came constantly to us. Luckily, we had a persistent team of writers and designers determined to tell their stories to the world.

We want Capsule to be a space for experimentation, where contributors could explore passion projects and bring them to life. And guess what? We have been heartened repeatedly by the dedication of our writers, the flexibility of our designers and the patience of our web editors as we created the inaugural issue of Capsule Magazine. We are setting the tone for the future.

So, what are the pieces about? You will meet writers who confront grief in their families. Others question the future of journalism. One writes about rising conservative thought in Asian-American communities. And many write about our favorite shows, video games and artists we don’t know of yet. You will meet pieces that will entertain and inform you, because though we are a magazine, we have the same goals as “regular journalism:” to inform, question, investigate and remember what we must remember.

This year has been a difficult one for journalists all over the world: from the wars in Ukraine and Gaza, attacks on press freedoms, and the shuttering of newsrooms nationwide, bold journalism is more necessary than ever. We hope to investigate more topics in the future, to create essays that will make you think and act. Capsule’s goal is to capture: fortunately, the world is quite big, and there is always something new to explore.

Expect more stories and essays; never any answers, but always more questions.

With love and respect,
Shane Dimapanat and Jasmine Kwok
